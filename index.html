Welcome to the College of Information, Entertainment, and Communication

Established in 2019, the College of Information Technology, Entertainment, and Communication (CITEC) is one of the newest additions to UBLC. It originated as a small department offering the Bachelor of Science in Information Technology and played a pivotal role as one of UBLCâ€™s pioneer programs back in 2011. The introduction of the Bachelor in Multimedia Arts program paved the way for the establishment of this thriving college.

CITEC is committed to nurturing critical thinkers, fostering research-oriented minds, and empowering individuals with creativity and innovation. We aim to shape our students into skilled professionals who can analyze complex organizational challenges, design innovative solutions, and contribute to technological advancements in the fields of information technology, entertainment, and communication.

At CITEC, we believe in unleashing the full potential of our students through experiential learning. Our dedicated and highly qualified faculty members serve as mentors, inspiring each student to achieve their best. We provide an environment conducive to learning because, at UBLC, **CITEC is where technology meets creativity**.

Discover boundless opportunities for growth, creativity, and technological excellence at the College of Information Technology, Entertainment, and Communication. Welcome to a dynamic learning experience!.

Academic Programs Offered

  • **Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT):** Focuses on computer utilization, software development, network administration, and managing IT infrastructure for business solutions.
  • **Bachelor of Science in Multimedia Arts (BMMA):** Equips students with knowledge and skills in multimedia design, production, and development, covering areas like animation, graphic design, web content, and video production.

CITEC Academic and Project Requirements

Capstone Project Requirements

The **Capstone Project or Thesis** is a mandatory final-year requirement for both the BSIT and BMMA programs, designed to synthesize all acquired knowledge and skills.

  • **Phase 1: Proposal and Feasibility Study** (Completed in Capstone 1 course) - Submission of project title, statement of the problem, literature review, and detailed project methodology.
  • **Phase 2: Development and Documentation** (Completed in Capstone 2 course) - Implementation of the working system (BSIT) or final creative product (BMMA), followed by the creation of a comprehensive technical/research paper.
  • **Phase 3: Final Oral Defense** - Formal presentation and defense of the project before a panel of experts and faculty.
  • **Final Output:** Must be a viable solution (BSIT) or a high-quality creative work (BMMA) that addresses a real-world need.

Student Rights (Per Education Act of 1982)

  • The right to receive relevant quality education and be given a copy of the **course syllabus** and the **College Student Handbook** upon admission.
  • The right to freely choose their field of study and **continue their course** up to graduation, except in cases of academic deficiency, or violation of disciplinary regulations.
  • The right to access their school records, the **confidentiality** of which the school shall maintain.
  • The right to the issuance of **official documents** (certificates, diplomas, transcript of records, etc.) within thirty days from request.
  • The right to **form, establish, join and participate in recognized organizations**.
  • The right to **free expression of opinions and suggestions**.

Student Responsibilities (Per Education Act of 1982)

  • Uphold the **academic integrity** of the school and endeavor to achieve academic excellence.
  • Promote and maintain the **peace and tranquility** of the school by observing rules and discipline.
  • Exercise rights responsibly, knowing they are **answerable for any infringement** or violation of the rights of others.
  • Never do anything that may **tarnish the name or reputation** of the school.
  • Must be familiar with the contents of this manual; **ignorance of the provisions** stated herein **does not excuse** any student from sanctions.

Uniform, ID, and Grooming

  • The school uniform is **worn on all class days**. P.E. uniform is worn only during P.E. classes.
  • An enrolled student is issued an **official identification card (ID)** which must always be worn while inside the campus and swiped when entering.
  • **Prohibited Attire (General):** Sando, plunging neckline, halter tops, see-through clothes, shorts/cycling/walking shorts (except for varsity practices), leggings or tights, tattered jeans, mini-skirts, and rubber/leather slippers.
  • **Hair and Accessories:** Bold hair colors are not allowed for male and female students. Male students are not allowed to wear earrings Tattoos must not be visible when wearing a school uniform.

Major Prohibitions (Immediate Disciplinary Action)

  • **Hazing** is strictly prohibited; violation of the Anti-Hazing Law is subject to severe disciplinary action and criminal prosecution.
  • **Illegal use, sale, possession, or distribution of any controlled drug** or narcotic on university property.
  • **Possession of firearms, knives, explosives, or other weapons** on university-owned or controlled property.
  • **Falsification of documents**.
  • **Gambling** inside the campus.

CLAYGO Policy

  • **CLAYGO** (Clean As You Go) is encouraged as part of one's daily routine and behavior.
  • This applies not just in the cafeteria, but to **ALL corners of the school**, including classrooms.
  • Non-compliance means infringement of the school regulation and is subject to disciplinary action.

Netiquette in Remote Learning

  • **Respect:** Treat teachers and classmates with respect at all times.
  • **Language:** Avoid using offensive and inappropriate language, including slang and text abbreviations.
  • **Yelling:** Avoid the use of capital letters which may be interpreted as "yelling".
  • **Privacy:** Be cautious when sharing information; ask permission before posting any information or materials online.
  • **Synchronous Classes:** Always **keep the video camera on** during the entire online class, listen attentively, and stay focused. Join the online class on time and mute the microphone once the class officially starts.

Academic Integrity in Remote Learning (Dishonesty)

Students must refrain from engaging in any form of academic dishonesty, which includes, but is not limited to:
    **Intentional sharing of login credentials** (UBian account).
  • **Sharing of any learning materials** produced and owned by the University/faculty to non-UB students without permission.
  • **eCheating:** Copying another student's work, using notes/devices during an exam without permission, or communicating with/helping another student during the exam.
